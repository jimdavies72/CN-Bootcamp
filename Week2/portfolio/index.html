Why I Joined Code Nation

I graduated form University back in 1995 with a degree in Business Information Systems and began my IT career as an 'Applications Support Officer' for a consultancy based out of South London. Within 12 months I progressed to 'Development Consultant' after showing great interest in software and applications development. I left the consultancy and joined a telecoms company based in Berkshire as a developer where I wrote 'CTI' telephony applications for call centres using VB6 and MS SQL Server. I later became Senior Developer' within the health sector.
Whilst in this job, a 'Development Manager' position arose and I was advised by various peers that if I wanted my career to progress then I should apply for this appointment. I was reticent as at that point all I wanted to do was 'write lines'. I caved to pressure and applied and was subsequently appointed into the position. This would prove to be my greatest career regret as now, instead of writing code which I loved, I was attending meetings, writing reports, managing first line and the department - My coding hours dropped from 37.5 hours to around 2-3.
I subsequently became a Project Manager, thinking that this would draw me closer to the 'coal face', in fact it only moved me further away. within 6 or so years I had lost all my technical skills, undertaking a job that offered me very little in the way of job satisfaction. Then in 2019, I reached my limit as I became quite ill - It was this that made me decide to quit project management for good. Shortly afterwards, we all know what happened on a global...
I kicked around for the best part of 2 years not knowing what I was going to do. I had thought about going back to development however at my age, I thought what chance did I have? I did some brief project work with some friends, but mostly again as PM with little dev involved. Then in August 2021, as if the fates poked me with a stick, I was playing a mobile phone game when an advert appeared offering courses in 'software development', so I thought 'you know what, Im going to apply for this, what is the worst that can happen?'. The rest is as they say history, here I am, back amongst the code where I belong.
